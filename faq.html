FAQ

What undergraduate programs does the Department offer?

The Department offers Bachelor of Science (BSc) programs in Computer Science, including Single-Major and Major-Minor options, as well as a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology.

Are there opportunities for internships and industry collaboration?

Yes, the Department collaborates with industry partners to provide internship opportunities for students. We believe in bridging the gap between academia and industry, offering students valuable real-world experience.

How is the curriculum structured, and what courses are offered?

The curriculum is designed to cover core computer science principles along with specialized courses. It includes programming, algorithms, databases, software engineering, and elective courses. Refer to the department website or academic advisors for detailed course information.

What resources are available in the department for students academic success

The department provides access to well-equipped computer labs, a library with relevant resources, online learning platforms, and collaboration spaces to support students in their academic endeavors.

How can I stay updated on departmental events and announcements?

Stay informed about departmental events, announcements, and news through the official department website, bulletin boards, and departmental newsletters. Follow the department's social media accounts for real-time updates.

What kind of networking opportunities are available for students within the department?

The department organizes networking events, guest lectures, and industry seminars where students can interact with professionals, alumni, and potential employers. These events provide valuable insights into the industry and potential career paths.

What support services are in place for students facing challenges in their coursework?

The department offers tutoring services, study groups, and peer mentoring to support students facing academic challenges. Faculty members are also available during office hours to provide additional assistance and guidance.

3How does the department prepare students for the job market?

The department provides career counseling, resume workshops, and mock interviews. Additionally, there are industry partnerships that facilitate internships and job placement. Career fairs are organized to connect students with potential employers.

Can students pursue joint degrees or minors with other departments or disciplines?

Yes, the department allows students to explore interdisciplinary studies. There are options for joint degrees and minors in areas such as mathematics, business, or any other field of interest to complement their computer science education.

Brief History

The Computer Science Department was founded as part of the University's strategic objective of developing the human resources needs in key technologies required to provide the foundation for the 21st century technological developmental base for the country. The Department has since its establishment, successfully graduated 49 out of 54 students (90.7%) from the first four pioneering groups. The current number of students in the Department as of December 2019 stands at 296, comprising 88 students at Level 100, 80 students at Level 200, 84 students at Level 300, and 44 students at Level 400.The population of female students is 18.5%. The Department currently has 8 full-time teaching staff (1 staff on PhD study leave), 6 part-time staff, 8 teaching assistants, 8 national service personnel, 3 technical supporting staff, and 1 administrative assistant. The state of the Department generally remains strong as a result of a combination of aggressive efforts to provide quality teaching, hiring new faculty, facilitate upgrading of teaching staff with MPhil degree to PhD, revitalize the undergraduate curriculum, and promoting short term visits by Visiting Scholars. Since its establishment, the Department has continued its efforts to run a broadly based Engineering discipline in software systems, computer networks, information processing and transmission, wireless communication system.
